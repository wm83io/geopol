## 4. Framework Additions

**Netanyahu Principal-Penetration Duration Variable (refines Day 74 mechanism):**

The Day 74 mechanism is correct: each Netanyahu-Trump direct call resets the US negotiating position toward Israeli dispositional framing. Day 76 data adds the critical refinement: penetration **decays within 24-48 hours** absent renewed Netanyahu contact. This is now observable from both sides. Israeli decision-makers are alarmed precisely because they can see the decay in real time. Operationally, this transforms Netanyahu's principal-channel from "intermittent override" to "high-frequency rebroadcast required." Two consequences. First, Netanyahu must increase contact frequency or lose the penetration effect — raising the operational risk of an overplayed hand (Trump A1 reaction against perceived pressure). Second, Israel's incentive to convert penetration into binding diplomatic insertions (sunset-clause modifications, dismantlement language) rises because operational pre-emption becomes the only durable instrument. The dual-track posture (insertions + pre-emption preparation) is the rational response to the decay problem.

**Asymmetric PA-Gap Lock-In (new structural mechanism):**

Day 74 modeled the PA-gap as bilateral and symmetric — both principals have positions their agents cannot bind. Day 76 data forces revision: the two PA-gaps are now asymmetric in kind. The US-side PA-gap is dispositionally unstable (Trump oscillates per A1; the "principal position" is the rolling output of his last high-status conversation). The Iran-side PA-gap is closing through public principal speech (Ghalibaf, Jafari, Hassanzadeh; Vahidi-consolidation). Per Putnam two-level games, this asymmetry has a specific consequence: Iran's audience costs for retreat are now larger than the US's, because Iran's principals have publicly committed and the US's has not. Iran's win-set is **smaller** than at MOU drafting; the US's win-set is **wider but volatile**. The joint feasible set is therefore narrower than at any point since the war began. Convergence requires either (a) Trump captured in a deal-preference state at the precise moment Iran is offered face-saving optic, or (b) external commitment device (China, Hajj focal point, multilateral guarantee) that allows both principals to accept smaller win-sets without bearing audience costs alone. The asymmetric lock-in makes Fork D' more probable than either Fork B or Fork A in the 30-day window — neither side can commit, but neither can walk away.

**Two-Track Hormuz Equilibrium (emergent, sub-structural):**

Iran-approved government-to-government transits (Qatar LNG x2, Pakistan-bound) coexist with commercial closure (Hapag-Lloyd/Maersk equivalents not operating).
