The framework currently under-prices these events by ignoring the convergence multiplier (single-tradition activation 1x vs. 4+-tradition activation 5-10x; convergence matrix §9).

The risk is that the framework treats each event as standalone rather than as activation node within an architecture.

## BS-New-2: Christian Zionist US Policy Vector (Disaggregation of Trump 2.0 Maximalist Frame)

**Category:** Constraint-layer L4 (faction misalignment) US-side input; modifier on US-Israel policy coherence assumption.

**The blind spot:** The framework's existing US constraint modeling treats Trump 2.0 as "maximalist on Israel" — a monolithic frame. This collapses an internally fractured coalition. The Christian Zionist policy vector (CUFI infrastructure, Huckabee ambassadorship, Project Esther, Heritage Foundation Christian-nationalist task force) operates as one faction within the Trump coalition. The Vance-Bannon-Carlson "America First" disengagement faction operates against it, particularly post-Gaza. Treating the US-Israel constraint as monolithic misses live intra-Republican variance that is decisive for any 2025-2028 scenario.

The structural decline of Christian Zionist political potency past 2028 is the secondary dimension. Inbari-Bumin / Barna polling: 18-29 evangelical Israel support fell from 68.9% in 2018 to 33.6% in 2021. White evangelical share of US population is in structural decline (PRRI: only 7% of 18-29-year-old Americans vs. 22% of 65+). Any framework projection past 2028 that assumes durable Christian Zionist-Republican fusion is mispriced.

**Why this is structural, not news-driven:** Coalition composition shifts on multi-year timescales. The framework currently lacks visibility into the maximalist-vs-disengagement intra-Republican fracture as a constraint variable. Day-to-day news flow (any given Huckabee statement, any given CUFI press release) cannot resolve this without a structural baseline.

**Indicators to monitor:**

| 1 | Huckabee policy statement contradicting State/DoD posture | T2 named-source reporting; State Department press briefings |

| 2 | Huckabee public statement on Greater Israel / Nile-to-Euphrates rhetoric | Reuters; The Forward; Crux |

| 3 | Mainline Protestant, Catholic, Orthodox positioning on Israel (counter-vector) | Holy Land Patriarchs statements; USCCB; Orthodox synods |

**Sub-probes:**

1. **Intra-Republican fracture intensity index.** Construct from public statement divergence on: (a) Iran posture (maximum pressure vs. negotiated arrangement), (b) Israel posture (Greater Israel rhetoric vs. status-quo), (c) Gaza policy (unconditional support vs. conditioning aid), (d) West Bank annexation (Smotrich-aligned support vs. opposition). Track quarterly.

2. **Christian Zionist policy delivery success rate.** Track Project Esther's stated proposals against implementation status. NYT May 2025: >50% implemented or called for. Quarterly delta is a leading indicator on coalition operational coherence.

3. **Evangelical generational replacement trajectory.** Annual update on 18-29 cohort Israel support. Crossing 25% threshold (downward) eliminates the demographic mass base; crossing 50% (upward, unlikely) would restore it. Either crossing is a framework-revision event.

4. **Counter-vector strength.** Mainline Protestant, Catholic, Orthodox positioning. Holy Land Patriarchs' January 2026 critique and the 14-nation diplomatic objection to Huckabee's February 2026 "Nile to Euphrates" comments are baseline data points.
