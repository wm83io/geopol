## Purpose

The framework's analytical power is bounded by what it cannot see. This appendix documents the structural blind spots identified through Day 76 analysis, assigns each a confidence level (how much we are flying blind), and issues standing weekly probe directives to surface data that would force framework revision.

The directives are not news-monitoring tasks. They are targeted intelligence probes aimed at specific signals that would either validate or break key framework assumptions. Each probe should be run once per week minimum, more frequently if adjacent data suggests movement.

**Day 70 audit note:** The framework's central trajectory inverted (Fork A → Fork B leading) within 72 hours of v2.6 publication, exposing a Fearon-mechanism blind spot — limited kinetic exchange treated as monotonic escalation rather than as type-revelation reopening the bargaining range. Audit reorganized Appendix B around the post-revelation deal architecture phase, integrating the game-theoretical apparatus (PA-gap, Schelling focal points, commitment devices, Slantchev feigning weakness, Weisiger dispositional reading, Powell shifting-power) as new BS layer.

**Day 76 audit note:** The Day 74 reading that the PA-gap had inverted in a hawkish US direction was overweight on durability. Day 76 data revealed that the Netanyahu Principal-Penetration mechanism is real but decays within 24-48 hours absent renewed contact, while Iran's PA-gap closed sharply through multi-vertex named-principal speech (Ghalibaf X post, Jafari, Hassanzadeh, Vahidi consolidation per ISW). The two PA-gaps are now asymmetric: US-side oscillates (A1 binding); Iran-side has closed and locked. Joint feasible set narrower than at any point since talks began. The framework's prediction problem has shifted from "which way will principals move" to "which actor breaks first." New BS-15 added to track this. BS-11 and BS-12 downgraded from Critical to High on substantive visibility improvements during the principal-resolution phase. BS-2 retired (Day 75 retirement criteria met).
