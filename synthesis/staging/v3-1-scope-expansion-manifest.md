### B8 — New Structural Mechanism: HEU Physical-State Information Rent

**Definition:** Both Variant A and Variant B of the Israeli-spoiler architecture, and the broader dismantlement-vs-deferral negotiating frame, assume Iranian HEU is a recoverable physical object accessible to extraction or destruction. The framework has not adjudicated four sub-states of the physical object:
- (a) HEU stocks materially intact in known locations (Fordow, Natanz, Pickaxe Mountain) — the implicit baseline assumption
- (b) HEU partially or wholly destroyed in Day 67 strikes — disclosed Iranian intransigence on "return clause" is performative absent recoverable inventory
- (c) HEU pre-emptively dispersed to undisclosed sites — Variant B operational planning faces an intelligence-collection problem disjoint from negotiating posture
- (d) HEU covertly transferred to Russia or China pre-strike or post-strike — the object is outside Iranian custody and outside US/Israeli reach

**Mechanism (Fearon-type):** This is an information rent on a physical fact, distinct from the bargaining rents the framework already maps (Slantchev feigning weakness on capability; Weisiger dispositional reading on intent). The negotiating sides may have asymmetric knowledge of the object's state. Iran's "HEU return clause" demand is consistent with sub-state (a), (b), (c), and (d) — it is a posture, not a revelation.

**Predictive implication:**
- Sub-state (a): Variant B feasible at currently modeled probability (12-18%); dismantlement language is operationally meaningful.
- Sub-state (b): Variant B inert; dismantlement is rhetorical; Israeli operational graduation threshold rises sharply because the operational objective is absent.
- Sub-state (c): Variant B requires preceding intelligence operation; timeline extends; Fork A path lengthens.
- Sub-state (d): Variant B requires Russian or Chinese cooperation to access HEU; Fork B-Russia revives at altered counterparty; Trump-Xi summit Iran-content becomes structurally about HEU custody, not just sequencing.

The framework cannot price Variant B without adjudicating which sub-state holds. The fact that no public US, Israeli, or IAEA statement has clarified physical state is itself a signal: either the IC has the answer and is withholding it (operational planning ongoing), or the IC does not have the answer (collection gap on Iran's most important physical asset).

**Add to:** Section 5 as Section 5.21. Cross-reference to Section 8.1 Variant B probability calibration as conditional on sub-state.

### F7 — Derived-Composite Methodology


Per B11. The framework distinguishes between **primitive** probability assessments (Forks A/B/C/D', Variants, tail register entries) and **derived** composites computed from them. Primitives are calibrated against evidence; derived values are functions of primitives with no independent calibration.

**Discipline:**
- Every derived value carries an explicit "DERIVED" label and its construction formula.
- Derived values never replace primitives in the matrix; they sit alongside.
- SITREP reporting includes derived headlines for decision-maker scannability but the analytical text references primitives.
- If a derived composite ever becomes the analytical object (driver of recommendations), the framework has lost discipline; refactor immediately.

**Why this matters:** The Kinetic Escalation Composite is the first derived value in the framework. Establishing the discipline now prevents future composites (e.g., "deterrence-failure composite", "regional-contagion composite") from being introduced without the same labeling and same primitive-priority rule.
