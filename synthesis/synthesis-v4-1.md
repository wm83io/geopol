## 2a. Methodological Note

The framework operates in the bargaining-model tradition (Fearon 1995; Powell 1999) extended with two-level games (Putnam 1988) and system-effects emergence (Jervis 1997). The materialist substrate is Engels-register, not vulgar-materialist: material conditions impose constraints; agents choose within them; the trajectory emerges as joint equilibrium. Path-dependence and lock-in follow Pierson (2000). Neoclassical realism (Rose 1998) names the IR-theoretic home for the "constraints condition, factions select within" structure. Full apparatus in `reference/costly-signaling-framework.md` and `appendix/appendix-c-methodology.md`. The framework predicts dominant strategies under joint constraints; it does not predict selection.
