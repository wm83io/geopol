## 2a. Methodological Note

The framework operates in the bargaining-model tradition (Fearon 1995; Powell 1999) extended with two-level games (Putnam 1988) and system-effects emergence (Jervis 1997). The materialist substrate is Engels-register, not vulgar-materialist: material conditions impose constraints; agents choose within them; the trajectory emerges as joint equilibrium. Path-dependence and lock-in follow Pierson (2000). Neoclassical realism (Rose 1998) names the IR-theoretic home for the "constraints condition, factions select within" structure. Full apparatus in `reference/costly-signaling-framework.md` and `appendix/appendix-c-methodology.md`. Two v4.2 discipline corrections (manifest S5, S6): trend states transition symmetrically (2+ independent cycles of contradicting evidence demotes VALIDATED → CONTESTED, matching the promotion threshold; a 30-day no-re-verifying-signal decay clause auto-demotes; `reference/strategic-trends.md` is authoritative); and source quality is distinct from source independence (multi-outlet pickup of one originating reporter or anonymous cluster is one source, and the -50% anonymous-source discount applies to the originating cluster regardless of outlet count; `reference/quality-sources.md` is authoritative). Principal identification is provisional and re-validated cycle-by-cycle via discriminating evidence (manifest S7); commitments absent discriminating evidence for 4+ cycles are flagged `inherited, not validated`. The framework predicts dominant strategies under joint constraints; it does not predict selection.
