## 2a. Methodological Note

The framework operates in the bargaining-model tradition (Fearon 1995; Powell 1999) extended with two-level games (Putnam 1988) and system-effects emergence (Jervis 1997). The materialist substrate is Engels-register, not vulgar-materialist: material conditions impose constraints; agents choose within them; the trajectory emerges as joint equilibrium. Path-dependence and lock-in follow Pierson (2000). Neoclassical realism (Rose 1998) names the IR-theoretic home for the "constraints condition, factions select within" structure. Full apparatus in `reference/costly-signaling-framework.md` and `appendix/appendix-c-methodology.md`. Three discipline corrections carry from v4.2 and are extended in v4.3: trend states transition symmetrically (2+ independent cycles of contradicting evidence demotes VALIDATED → CONTESTED; a 30-day no-re-verifying-signal decay clause auto-demotes; `reference/strategic-trends.md` is authoritative); source quality is distinct from source independence (multi-outlet pickup of one originating cluster is one source; the -50% anonymous-source discount applies to the originating cluster regardless of outlet count; `reference/quality-sources.md` is authoritative); and principal identification is provisional and re-validated cycle-by-cycle via discriminating evidence, with replacement attributions entering at provisional confidence rather than inheriting the halo of the attribution they replace (the v4.3 extension; `reference/quality-sources.md` Source-Independence Discipline replacement-attribution addendum). Commitments absent discriminating evidence for 5+ cycles are routed to provisional demotion, not re-flagged indefinitely. The framework predicts dominant strategies under joint constraints; it does not predict selection.
