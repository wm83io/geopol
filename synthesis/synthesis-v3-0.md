## 6. Bargaining Theory and Limited War — NEW (v3.0 structural mechanism)

This section integrates Fearon-Slantchev-Schelling-Reiter-Weisiger-Powell as a structural mechanism, not as commentary. The apparatus is now operative in framework predictions.

### 6.1 Fearon's Three Rationalist Mechanisms (the canonical decomposition)

Fearon ("Rationalist Explanations for War," IO 1995): war is ex post inefficient; for any war there exists Pareto-superior settlement. Three mechanisms survive scrutiny as explanations for why ex ante settlement fails:

1. **Private information about capabilities/resolve combined with incentives to misrepresent.** Quiet diplomacy cannot reveal type because each side has incentives to bluff for bargaining advantage. Costly signals required.
2. **Commitment problems**, especially under shifting power: rising B cannot credibly commit not to revise bargain after gaining strength; declining A may prefer war today to worse bargain tomorrow.
3. **Issue indivisibility** — Fearon's empirically-thin third mechanism.

**Application Iran 2026.** Mechanism 1 dominated through Feb 28, 2026: each side's true resolve, capability, and cost-tolerance was private information; both had incentives to bluff. The June 2025 Twelve-Day War and Feb 28 strikes resolved Mechanism 1 substantially. Mechanism 2 is the Powell preventive-war risk: US/Israel may believe Iran is weak now and will recover; "bargain now" preference produces war preference rather than deal preference even under full information. BS-14 cross-reference. Mechanism 3 inactive.

### 6.2 Slantchev's Principle of Convergence (the master mechanism for current phase)

Slantchev ("Principle of Convergence," APSR 2003): *"If war results from disagreement about relative strength, then it ends when opponents learn enough about each other. Learning occurs when information is revealed by strategically manipulable negotiation behavior and nonmanipulable battlefield outcomes... warfare ceases to be useful when it loses its informational content, and that belief in defeat (victory) is not necessary to terminate (initiate) hostilities. In most cases states settle before a clear military victory."*

**Operational reading.** Battlefield outcomes are *non-manipulable* information; bargaining offers are *strategically manipulable*. States learn from both. The June 2025 Twelve-Day War, Feb 28 strikes, and Day 67 Project Freedom exchange exhausted high-value information rents on resolve and capability. The April 7-8 Pakistan-mediated 10-point ceasefire framework was rejected as a starting point in January 2026; it became plausible only after kinetic exchange exhausted information rents on both sides. This is the convergence principle operating.

**Day 70 PM reading.** Second iteration of mutual demonstration. Iran tested IRGC Navy combined-arms anti-ship capability against three US destroyers. CENTCOM tested layered defense. Both calibrated below escalation thresholds: no high-value asset targeted (no carrier; no Iranian leadership); no commercial vessel sinking; no mass-casualty event; no mainland US infrastructure cyber Stage 3. The information content of further fighting remains low. Per Slantchev: warfare has lost informational content; the question is now coordination, not resolve.

### 6.3 Sinking Costs vs. Tying Hands (Fearon 1997 / Slantchev 2005)

Fearon ("Signaling Foreign Policy Interests," JCR 1997): two costly-signal mechanisms:
- **Tying hands**: creating audience/reputational costs payable *only if* leader fails to follow through. Spence-style.
- **Sinking costs**: paying ex-ante costs (mobilization, treasure spent) regardless of whether war occurs.

Slantchev ("Military Coercion in Interstate Crises," APSR 2005): mobilization simultaneously sinks costs AND ties hands by changing probability of victory. Bluffing possible in equilibrium contra Fearon 1997.

**Application Iran 2026.** Trump's signaling is dispositional/personal, not institutional. Senate ratification not on offer. Audience costs unreliable. *Sinking costs (forward-deployed CSGs, Eisenhower available but undeployed, ~$25B war cost baseline, 13 KIA)* are more probative than *tying hands (public statements)* in this case. Iranian sinking costs comparable: 6 small craft Day 67 + Day 70 PM Qeshm/Bandar Abbas damage + Iranian regime fiscal compression ~$170M/day.

**v3.0 finding.** Maintaining forward US naval presence is now signaling of deal-track resolve, not war-track resolve. Withdrawal would signal weakening resolve per Slantchev; presence is sunk cost that ties hands to MOU follow-through. Eisenhower non-deployment 4 days post-Day-67 + 1 day post-Day-70-PM is calibrated sinking-cost signal: enough to maintain Stage-2 demonstration capacity, not enough to compel Fork A activation under inadvertent-escalation conditions.
