## 1. Purpose and Scope

The two standalone eschatology documents describe traditions in isolation. They answer "what does Jewish Religious Zionism want at the Temple Mount" and "what does Iranian Twelver state Mahdism want at Quds Day" separately. They do not answer the operational question that matters for risk pricing: when multiple eschatological actors target the same trigger event from opposite sides, what is the escalation multiplier?

This document maps that intersection.

It is a structural reference, not a SITREP. It updates on version table only. It does not get rewritten on daily news flow. The convergence architecture is stable across months; the activation states inside it are tracked through probes and SITREPs.

## 2. The Convergence Problem

Standalone eschatology is not the operational risk. The risk is convergence: events that activate Jewish messianic, Christian dispensationalist, Sunni Mahdist, and Shia Twelver vanguards simultaneously, in the same geographic space, with mutually exclusive endgames.

Four characteristics define a convergence event:

1. **Geographic singularity.** A single location is sacred coordinate in multiple traditions.
2. **Trigger reciprocity.** Action by one tradition's vanguard is the prophetic confirmation that other traditions' vanguards have been waiting for. The settler ascent that signals *athalta d'geulah* to the Hardal wing is the Al-Aqsa desecration that signals *Toofan al-Aqsa* to Hamas and the *fitna* preceding the Mahdi to Sunni mainstream.
3. **Incompatible endgames.** Jewish messianism requires gentile subordination under restored Davidic monarchy. Christian dispensationalism requires mass Jewish conversion or destruction at Tribulation. Sunni Mahdism requires global submission to a restored caliphate. Twelver Mahdism requires the Hidden Imam's emergence and global rule from Kufa. These cannot coexist as victory states.
4. **State-level escalation pathway.** Activation crosses from non-state vanguard to state policy via existing political alliances (Religious Zionism in Israeli cabinet; CUFI in US Republican coalition; IRGC in Iranian state; HTS in Damascus).

When all four are present, the event is not a flashpoint. It is a strategic trigger with multi-domain consequences. The framework's existing escalation model treats trigger events as single-tradition activations and systematically under-prices convergence.

## 9. Cross-Tradition Escalation Multipliers

The framework's existing escalation model treats trigger events as single-tradition activations. The convergence multiplier captures interaction effects.

### 9.1 Interaction logic

Each additional activated tradition does not add linearly to escalation; it multiplies. Two reasons:

1. **Legitimating cover**: each tradition's vanguard provides legitimating cover for the others' escalation. A Hilltop Youth attack on the Dome of the Rock activates Jewish Hardal vindication, dispensationalist rapture-imminence framing, pan-Sunni street mobilization, and Twelver Shia regime mobilization simultaneously. Each tradition's escalation argument is reinforced by the others' activation.

2. **Veto-player blocking**: each tradition's domestic political base provides veto-player blocking against de-escalation. Israeli centrists cannot pull back Religious Zionist ministers when their base sees Mahdist mobilization on the other side; US Republicans cannot pull back Christian Zionist organizational power when evangelical media sees Al-Aqsa Flood iconography; Saudi normalizers cannot proceed when pan-Sunni street demands Al-Aqsa response. Convergence locks in escalation at the political level.
