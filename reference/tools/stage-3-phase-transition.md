# Iran 2026 Conceptual Tools — Stage 3: Phase Transition Tracking

*Application window: 2027–2030 long cycle. Purpose: track whether the post-war configuration stabilizes as multiplex order, transitions to bipolar (US-China), or settles as tripolar form. Operationalize through measurable indicators on multi-year horizons.*

---

## How to Use This Catalog

Stage 3 abandons the question "what is happening in Iran" for the question "what global order is emerging." The Iran 2026 conflict is treated not as a discrete event but as a triggering or accelerating moment in a deeper phase transition that began with the 2008 financial crisis and progressed through Crimea 2014, Ukraine 2022, and the Trump 2.0 inauguration. The catalog asks which post-war configuration stabilizes:

| Configuration | Description | Key signal |
|---|---|---|
| **Multiplex (Acharya)** | Multiple overlapping regional orders; plural globalisms; no single hegemon | BRICS+ membership growth + persistent US bilateral retreat |
| **Bipolar (US-China)** | Two coherent blocs with hardened economic and security boundaries | Decoupling indices crossing irreversibility thresholds in chips, finance, energy |
| **Tripolar (US-China-Russia/civilizational bloc)** | Three poles with India/Brazil/Saudi Arabia as wildcards | Russia-China-Iran consolidation + persistent Indian non-alignment |
| **Restored hegemonic** | US re-asserts post-war primacy | Treaty ratification recovery + dollar share rebound + alliance formalization |

The catalog is organized into four operational tracks that produce these signals:

| Track | Tools | Indicator |
|---|---|---|
| **A. Order-architecture** | Multiplex order; End of rules-based order; Multipolarity vs. multiplex | ICJ/ICC engagement patterns; treaty ratification rates |
| **B. Currency and reserves** | Dollar weaponization fatigue; Two capitalisms; Civilization-state | Yuan share of oil settlement; gold share of reserves |
| **C. Civilizational-cultural** | Polycrisis; Carl Schmitt revival; Religious nationalism; Memory politics | Civilization-state self-identification; bloc cultural-policy alignment |
| **D. Complex-systems** | Phase transitions; Self-organized criticality; Dragon kings; Antifragility | Long-tail event distribution; bifurcation indicators |

## TRACK A: Order Architecture

### A.1 Multiplex Order

**Originator.** Amitav Acharya (2014, 2017, 2024); developed in *International Affairs* "critical multiplexity" article (2026).

**Definition.** Not a new monolithic order but a layered configuration with multiple regional orders, plural globalisms, and the global South as agent. Distinct from multipolarity (which retains the great-power-balancing frame) by recognizing that civilizational, regional, and functional orders coexist without hierarchy.

**Primary insight.** "Multipolar" is the wrong frame; what is emerging is multiplex — multiple overlapping orders with no single hegemon. The Iran 2026 war does not produce a return to US hegemony nor a clean transition to Chinese hegemony, but a fragmented order in which Trump's "hostilities terminated" can stand because no single normative authority can contradict it.

**Stage 3 leverage note.** Operationalization requires tracking three metrics annually: (1) **Number of overlapping regional security architectures** with non-trivial membership cross-cutting bloc lines (BRICS+ membership, SCO, ASEAN+, GCC integrated air defense, AUKUS, Quad, EU strategic autonomy formations); (2) **Currency multiplicity in major commodity trade** — yuan, ruble, AED, INR shares of oil, gas, food, fertilizer trade independently of dollar; (3) **Treaty ratification rates** for global instruments (climate, ocean, nuclear, biological, AI governance). Multiplex confirmation: high regional-architecture multiplicity + high commodity currency multiplicity + falling treaty ratification rates. Bipolar confirmation: bloc consolidation reduces architecture multiplicity; commodity trade splits between two currency zones; treaty engagement bifurcates.

---

### A.2 End of Rules-Based Order

**Originator.** G. John Ikenberry (multiple works since 2018); contested updates from realist and global-South perspectives.

**Definition.** Ikenberry: the rule-based liberal order is decaying under internal (Trump rejection of multilateral institutions) and external (BRICS+ rise, civilizational-state contestation) pressure. The decay is not a single collapse but progressive erosion of (a) institutional centrality (UN, WTO, IMF), (b) normative consensus (rules of war, sovereignty, human rights), and (c) US willingness to bear the costs of order maintenance.

**Primary insight.** The order's demise is gradual and reversible until specific institutional thresholds are crossed. Past those thresholds, restoration requires hegemonic capacity that may not be available.

**Stage 3 leverage note.** Five indicators to track quarterly: (1) **US treaty ratification rate** (ratifications per year, multi-year moving average) — historical baseline ~10–15/year, recent years 2–5/year, post-Iran-2026 trajectory critical; (2) **WTO dispute settlement system functioning** — Appellate Body restoration or continued paralysis; (3) **UNSC permanent-member coordination** on enforceable resolutions vs. veto patterns; (4) **ICJ/ICC engagement** (US, Russia, China, Iran appearance and compliance rates); (5) **G7 vs. G20 substantive output ratio** — declining G20 output relative to G7 indicates bloc-formation; rising G20 indicates persistent multiplex frame.

---

### A.3 Multipolarity vs. Polypolarity vs. Interpolarity

**Definition.** Three competing conceptualizations of post-unipolar order. *Multipolar*: multiple coherent great powers competing; *polypolar*: many actors of varying types (states, corporations, transnational networks) with no clear hierarchy; *interpolar*: multipolar structure overlaid with deep economic interdependence that constrains rivalry.

**Primary insight.** The framing choice has policy implications. Multipolar framings prescribe alliance-formation; polypolar framings prescribe network-management; interpolar framings prescribe interdependence-preservation.

**Stage 3 leverage note.** Track which framing dominates US, EU, Chinese, and Indian official strategic-document language. Convergence on a single framing across major powers indicates emerging consensus on post-Iran-war order architecture; divergence indicates continued instability. Indicator: presence of "multipolar" vs. "multiplex" vs. "polycentric" language in (a) US National Security Strategy (2027 expected); (b) EU Strategic Compass updates; (c) Chinese white papers on global governance; (d) Indian foreign-policy doctrine documents.

### A.4 Civilization-State

**Originator.** Lucian Pye (1990); Martin Jacques (2009, 2011); recent revivals by Huiyun Feng and Adrian Pabst.

**Definition.** A polity grounding legitimacy not in nation-statehood but in claims to civilizational continuity. China, Russia, India, Turkey, Iran, and increasingly the US under MAGA all exhibit civilization-state self-conceptions. Sovereignty is "separate from system" — civilizational claims authorize exception to international norms.

**Primary insight.** Civilization-states do not interact with the rule-based liberal order on its terms; they interpret international law through civilizational frames that authorize exception. The number of self-identifying civilization-states is a leading indicator of rules-based-order erosion.

**Stage 3 leverage note.** Track presence of civilizational-frame language in (a) major-power strategic documents; (b) educational curricula; (c) public discourse via official media. Iran's post-war regime uses Mojtaba's lineage continuity as civilizational-state legitimation; Russia's "Russian World" doctrine; China's "Chinese characteristics"; Trump's MAGA framing as civilizational restoration. Indicator threshold: when civilizational frames appear in 4+ major-power official discourse simultaneously, multiplex order has replaced rules-based order in the dominant elite mental model.

## TRACK B: Currency and Reserves

### B.1 Dollar Weaponization Fatigue / Reserve-Currency Erosion

**Definition.** Repeated coercive use of the dollar creates structural incentive for reserve diversification. Slow erosion (dollar share of reserves fell from ~73% in 2001 to roughly 54–58% by 2024–2025) with potential non-linear acceleration when threshold events occur. The Iran 2026 war is one such threshold event.

**Primary insight.** Each weaponization episode adds a discrete increment to the diversification incentive. Central-bank gold purchases ran at 1,045 tonnes in 2024 (World Gold Council), the third consecutive year above 1,000 tonnes. The trend pre-dates Iran 2026; the war is a discrete accelerant whose multi-year effect can be measured.

**Stage 3 leverage note.** Quarterly tracking via IMF COFER data + World Gold Council central-bank surveys + BIS Triennial Survey. Three signal levels:

| Indicator | Pre-war baseline | Threshold of concern | Threshold of irreversibility |
|---|---|---|---|
| Dollar share of allocated reserves | 58% | <55% | <50% |
| Yuan share of allocated reserves | 2% | >5% | >8% |
| Gold share of allocated reserves | 17% | >22% | >27% |
| Yuan share of oil settlement | <5% | >12% | >25% |
| BRICS Pay / CIPS daily volume vs. SWIFT | <5% | >15% | >35% |

Crossing two or more "threshold of irreversibility" levels by 2030 confirms reserve-currency phase transition. The phase transition does not require dollar collapse; it requires loss of dollar's coercive premium.

### B.2 Two Capitalisms

**Originator.** Branko Milanovic (*Capitalism, Alone*, 2019).

**Definition.** Post-1989 capitalism comes in two forms: Western "liberal-meritocratic" capitalism and Asian "political capitalism" (party-state direction, selective markets, instrumental law). Both capitalist; their conflict is over which model dominates.

**Primary insight.** The conflict is not capitalism vs. alternative but two structurally different capitalisms — making the contest one of state capacity, technological dominance, and ideological coherence.

**Stage 3 leverage note.** Track convergence vs. divergence between the two capitalisms across five domains: (1) **Industrial policy intensity** — DPA Title III usage in US; equivalent measures in EU; Made in China 2025 successor programs; (2) **State-owned enterprise share of GDP** in major economies; (3) **Capital-account openness** indices; (4) **Antitrust enforcement patterns** — US and EU vs. Chinese SAMR; (5) **Technology-transfer regimes** — export controls, foreign-investment screening, research-collaboration restrictions. If the two capitalisms continue to diverge through 2030, the bipolar configuration is more probable than the multiplex; if they converge (e.g., US adopts more state-direction; China loosens party-state direction), the multiplex frame holds.

---

### B.3 Geoeconomics as Statecraft

**Originator.** Robert Blackwill and Jennifer Harris (*War by Other Means*, 2016).

**Definition.** Seven instruments — trade policy, investment policy, sanctions, cyber, aid, monetary policy, energy/commodity policy — used systematically to advance geopolitical objectives. War-by-other-means.

**Primary insight.** US foreign policy "too often reaches for the gun over the purse"; rivals (China, Russia, Iran) routinely deploy geoeconomic instruments first. Post-Iran-2026 question: does the US institutionalize geoeconomic capacity at scale comparable to the kinetic capacity it deployed?

**Stage 3 leverage note.** Track US geoeconomic institutional buildout: (1) **Treasury OFAC capacity** — staffing, technology, multilateral coordination; (2) **DFC and EXIM scale** vs. Chinese Belt and Road Initiative comparable instruments; (3) **Export control regime** — BIS staffing, Entity List dynamics, multilateral export control coordination; (4) **CHIPS Act and IRA implementation** durability; (5) **Tariff regime** — strategic vs. transactional usage. If US geoeconomic capacity grows post-war, the bipolar/restored-hegemonic configurations gain probability; if it stagnates while Chinese and BRICS+ instruments expand, multiplex order is confirmed.

---

### B.4 Chokepoint Capitalism — Long-Cycle Form

**Definition.** Geographic and infrastructural chokepoints (Hormuz, Suez, Bab-el-Mandeb, undersea cables, payment rails, semiconductor supply chains) can be *captured* for rent extraction. The chokepoint creates structural monopoly leverage.

**Primary insight.** The long-cycle question is whether new chokepoints emerge to replace the ones that lost coercive premium. Post-Iran-2026, dollar-clearing chokepoints have lost some coercive premium; semiconductor chokepoints (TSMC, ASML, Applied Materials) have gained; rare-earth and battery-mineral chokepoints (China-dominated) have gained.

**Stage 3 leverage note.** Build a chokepoint inventory tracking which actors control which nodes:

| Chokepoint | Pre-2026 controller | 2030 controller | Coercive premium |
|---|---|---|---|
| Dollar clearing | US Treasury | Erosion expected | Falling |
| SWIFT messaging | EU/US co-governance | Erosion via CIPS, BRICS Pay | Falling |
| Semiconductor lithography | ASML (NL) | Same; possible new entrants | Stable-rising |
| Leading-edge chip fab | TSMC (TW) | TSMC + US/JP/DE diversification | Stable |
| Rare earths processing | China | China | Stable-rising |
| Battery minerals | China + DRC + Indonesia | More diversified | Stable |
| LNG export capacity | US + Qatar + Australia | More entrants | Falling |
| Hormuz transit | Iran (de facto) + GCC | TBD | Variable |

Movement in coercive-premium row across 2027–2030 reveals the long-cycle redistribution of geoeconomic power.

## TRACK C: Civilizational and Cultural

### C.1 Polycrisis — Long-Cycle Form

**Originator.** Jean-Claude Juncker (early 2010s); Adam Tooze (2022–2024); Cascade Institute formalization (Lawrence/Janzwood/Homer-Dixon, *Global Sustainability* 2024).

**Definition.** A configuration in which simultaneous, causally interlinked crises across distinct systems interact such that aggregate harm exceeds the sum of components. Crucially, the simultaneity reflects shared underlying drivers. Cascade Institute formalization: crises *entangle* through (a) common stressors, (b) cross-domain cascades, (c) shared response capacities, (d) feedback loops.

**Primary insight.** Polycrisis analysis is multidisciplinary by necessity. Single-domain analyses systematically miss the coupling and produce systematically biased forecasts.

**Stage 3 leverage note.** Track whether the Iran 2026 polycrisis configuration (oil + food + dollar + constitutional + Russian-Ukraine + Chinese property + climate) resolves or compounds. Indicators: (1) **Compound stress events** — frequency of events affecting 3+ domains simultaneously (2027 baseline establishment, then year-over-year tracking); (2) **Cross-domain response capacity** — IMF, WHO, IEA, FAO coordination metrics; (3) **Polycrisis policy instruments** — emergence (or absence) of cross-domain governance frameworks. If polycrisis frequency rises through 2030 without commensurate response-capacity buildout, the international system enters chronic-crisis equilibrium that itself becomes the order.

---

### C.2 Carl Schmitt's Friend/Enemy Distinction Revival

**Definition.** Schmitt's *Concept of the Political* (1927): the political is constituted by the existential distinction between friend and enemy. A polity that cannot make this distinction loses sovereignty. Post-2016 revival in geopolitical analysis (Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy entry; *Miskatonian* "Sacred Enmity and Political Identity," 2025).

**Primary insight.** In moments of system stress, liberal-procedural politics gives way to Schmittian friend-enemy logic — and actors who can wield the distinction unilaterally accrue sovereign power.

**Stage 3 leverage note.** Track the prevalence of friend-enemy framing in major-power official discourse. Indicators: (1) Use of "enemy" language in domestic political discourse (US, EU, India, Israel) — historically rare in liberal-democratic discourse, increasingly common; (2) Sovereign-decision moments (executive bypasses of legislative or judicial constraint, justified by exception); (3) Religious-nationalist framing of geopolitical conflict. Schmittian discourse penetration is a leading indicator of constitutional-democratic regression and of bipolar bloc formation.

### C.3 Religious Nationalism as Strategic Variable

**Definition.** Religious-nationalist mobilization changes regime calculus by (a) raising compromise costs (theological commitments cannot be split-the-difference negotiated), (b) providing transnational solidarity networks, (c) generating eschatological framings that authorize sacrifice.

**Primary insight.** Religious-nationalist regimes face *higher* compellence costs because public capitulation reads as theological defeat. Religious-nationalist movements within secular states constrain those states' negotiating space.

**Stage 3 leverage note.** Track religious-nationalist consolidation across the four configurations: (1) **Iran** — Mojtaba's clerical-political fusion vs. internal-pragmatist resistance; Shia vs. nationalist factions; (2) **US** — Christian Zionist + MAGA fusion durability; influence on Iran/Israel policy; (3) **Israel** — religious-Zionist coalition durability post-Iran-war; (4) **India** — Hindutva consolidation under Modi successor. Religious-nationalist consolidation across all four reduces the multiplex configuration's stability and increases bipolar/civilizational-bloc probability.

### C.4 Memory Politics / Warped Mourning — Long-Cycle Form

**Originator.** Alexander Etkind (*Warped Mourning*, 2013).

**Definition.** Societies that fail to properly mourn political dead develop pathological politics in which trauma is endlessly reactivated as legitimating myth. The long-cycle implication: martyrologies institutionalized today shape political possibilities for decades.

**Primary insight.** Once Khamenei's martyrology is curricularized in Iranian schools, anchored in physical memorialization (the "Martyrs of the Epic Struggle" mural), and ritualized in Friday prayer, it becomes a multi-decade structural feature of Iranian politics.

**Stage 3 leverage note.** Multi-year monitoring of martyrological institutionalization across Iran and adjacent cases. Iranian indicators: curriculum, monumental architecture, anniversary commemoration, political iconography. Russian indicators: WWII memory politics, post-Ukraine memorialization. US indicators: January 6 memory politics, Trump-era political martyrology. Each successful institutionalization increases the future political space's path-dependence and reduces the probability of liberal-restoration configurations.

## TRACK D: Complex-Systems Dynamics

### D.1 Phase Transitions in International Order

**Definition.** International orders exhibit *phase transitions* — discrete reorganizations of alliance structure, settlement architecture, and norm regime, separated by long periods of relative stability.

**Primary insight.** Long stability masks growing latent stress; transition can be rapid once threshold is crossed. The 2022–2030 period is plausibly the rolling phase transition out of the unipolar moment.

**Stage 3 leverage note.** The Iran 2026 war is a candidate triggering event (analogous to 1956 Suez for British/French decline). Post-2030 retrospective will confirm or disconfirm. Stage 3 indicators: (1) **Order-defining events** — frequency and consequentiality of moments comparable to Iran 2026; (2) **Rebalancing rate** — speed of alliance and economic restructuring; (3) **Norm-regime turnover** — replacement of post-1945 norms with new conventions. Phase transition completes when (a) a new dominant configuration is recognizable, (b) major powers' strategic documents reflect that configuration, and (c) secondary powers calibrate to it.

---

### D.2 Self-Organized Criticality / Power-Law Conflict Distributions

**Originator.** Per Bak (1987); applied to conflict by Lars-Erik Cederman (2003) and Bohorquez et al. (2009).

**Definition.** Slowly driven threshold systems organize toward critical states where event sizes follow power-law distributions. Cederman: interstate war casualty distributions follow this pattern; Bohorquez: insurgency event sizes do too.

**Primary insight.** Catastrophic war size is the predictable upper tail of a system organizing toward criticality. The next 2× event is roughly equally likely as any 1× event. The international system's organization toward criticality is itself the long-cycle dynamic.

**Stage 3 leverage note.** Construct a 2027–2030 conflict-event database. Power-law fit of event sizes (casualties, economic cost, geographic scope) will reveal whether the international system is at criticality. If the power-law exponent is shallow (more weight in tail), system is at criticality and large events are probable. If steep, system has stabilized post-Iran-2026. The exponent value is itself the order-architecture diagnostic.

### D.3 Dragon Kings

**Originator.** Didier Sornette (2009).

**Definition.** Distinct from Taleb's "black swans," dragon kings are extreme outliers that *do* exhibit precursors and *can* be partially predicted because they emerge from specific bifurcations, phase transitions, and positive-feedback mechanisms.

**Primary insight.** Some catastrophes are knowable in advance if you watch for specific dynamics — coupling, tipping, self-reinforcement.

**Stage 3 leverage note.** Three dragon-king candidates for 2027–2030: (1) **Reserve-currency phase transition** — non-linear acceleration of dollar erosion if a triggering event (further sanctions weaponization, US fiscal crisis, political instability) occurs; (2) **Regional alignment cascade** — UAE OPEC exit triggering Saudi Red Sea pivot triggering Egyptian/Jordanian repositioning triggering broader Sunni-bloc fragmentation; (3) **Constitutional-order phase transition** — May 2026 War Powers maneuver normalizing executive bypass and triggering broader retrogression cascade. Each carries identifiable bifurcation indicators that can be monitored.

---

### D.4 Antifragility

**Originator.** Nassim Taleb (2012).

**Definition.** Antifragile systems gain from disorder, distinct from robust (resists shock) or resilient (recovers). Antifragility comes from optionality, redundancy, decentralization, skin-in-the-game.

**Primary insight.** Centralized, optimized systems are fragile. Decentralized, redundant systems can be antifragile. The long-cycle question: which actors and structures emerge stronger from the post-Iran-2026 disorder?

**Stage 3 leverage note.** Antifragility audit at 2030: which actors gained from the 2026–2030 disorder? Candidates: (a) **IRGC** — mosaic structure designed for decapitation antifragility; tested and confirmed; (b) **BRICS+ infrastructure** — gained from each weaponization episode; (c) **Defense-industrial firms** — Palantir, Anduril gained from threat-framework dependency; (d) **Civilization-state regimes** — gained ontological-security reinforcement from confrontation. Conversely: (a) **US executive-legislative balance** — fragility revealed and amplified; (b) **Rules-based order institutions** — fragility revealed; (c) **Alliance commitments based on credibility** — fragility revealed. The antifragile vs. fragile sort post-2030 is the durable strategic legacy.

### D.5 Lindy Effect Applied to Institutional Resilience

**Originator.** Reformulation by Taleb of older folk-wisdom.

**Definition.** Expected remaining life of a non-perishable item is proportional to its current age. Old institutions accumulate adaptations to known stresses; novel institutions are fragile.

**Primary insight.** Lindy-old institutions outlast their current crises with higher probability than Lindy-young institutions.

**Stage 3 leverage note.** Lindy ranking of post-2030 institutions:

| Institution | Age (2030) | Lindy expectation |
|---|---|---|
| Islamic Republic of Iran | 51 years | High survivability |
| War Powers Resolution | 57 years | Moderate (formally old; recently bypassed) |
| BRICS Pay | <5 years | Lindy-fragile |
| mBridge | <5 years | Lindy-fragile |
| Saudi-US security relationship | ~85 years | High (but formally tested 2026) |
| Rules-based order conventions | ~80 years | High (but normatively contested) |
| US-Taiwan implicit guarantee | ~50 years | Moderate-high |
| US dollar reserve dominance | ~80 years | High (with declining coercive premium) |

Lindy-fragile institutions face higher mortality through 2030; Lindy-strong institutions are more likely to survive but may persist with degraded function. The ratio of Lindy-strong to Lindy-fragile in the post-2030 architecture indicates whether the order is novel or restored.

## Caveats

The Stage 3 catalog operates on multi-year horizons and produces probabilistic rather than deterministic forecasts. Its limitations: (1) the indicator thresholds are calibrated to the current best understanding of regime dynamics and may need adjustment as the post-2026 period unfolds; (2) some indicators (BRICS Pay daily volume, mBridge deployment status) require disclosure that is not consistent across central banks; (3) the four configurations are not mutually exclusive — hybrid forms (e.g., bipolar with multiplex peripheries) are possible and may dominate; (4) the catalog assumes no third-order shock (climate-driven mass displacement, AI-driven economic restructuring, pandemic) overrides the Iran-2026-induced trajectory. Such shocks would require Stage 3 catalog revision, not just indicator updates.

The catalog's central claim is that the Iran 2026 war is best understood not as a discrete conflict but as a triggering or accelerating moment in a deeper phase transition that began before 2026 and will play out over the subsequent decade. The Stage 3 indicators measure that phase transition; the stage itself does not end until the post-transition order is recognizable.
