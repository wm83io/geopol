# Iran 2026 Conceptual Tools — Stage 2: Hysteresis Assessment

*Application window: Q3 2026, conditional on Hormuz reopening or sustained ceasefire. Purpose: identify which 2026 reorganizations are reversible and which are locked in. The locked-in subset is the durable strategic legacy.*

---

## How to Use This Catalog

Stage 2 begins when the kinetic phase ends, whether through ceasefire, frozen-conflict drift, or rebranding. The analytical question shifts from "what is happening" to "what does not unwind." The catalog's central tool is **hysteresis**: the property of systems whose return path differs from their forward path. Several 2026 reorganizations are visibly under way (UAE OPEC exit, Saudi Red Sea pivot, BRICS payments capex, gold-reserve buildup, constitutional retrogression). Stage 2 sorts these into reversible versus locked-in.

The catalog is organized around four reorganization domains:

| Domain | Key reorganization | Hysteresis question |
|---|---|---|
| **Energy geography** | UAE OPEC exit; Saudi Red Sea pivot | Does Hormuz reopen restore prior dependency, or do alternative routes lock in? |
| **Financial architecture** | BRICS payments capex; gold-reserve buildup | Once capex is sunk, do alternative rails hold, or does dollar dominance restore? |
| **Regime political settlement** | Iranian Mojtaba succession; rentier consolidation | Does the IRGC triangle persist, or do pragmatist factions reassert? |
| **US institutional residue** | WPR precedent; competitive-authoritarian episode | Are May 2026 maneuvers reversed, or do they become baseline? |

Each tool below carries a definition, primary insight, and a Stage 2 leverage note specifying the hysteresis indicator the tool generates.

## DOMAIN 1: Energy Geography

### 1.1 Tipping Points / Hysteresis

**Definition.** Systems that change state under increasing stress often exhibit *hysteresis*: the path back requires far more reversal than the path that produced the change. The system has *memory*; restoring the input variable to its pre-shift value does not restore the output state.

**Primary insight.** "Just go back to normal" is unavailable after critical thresholds. The post-crisis configuration is path-dependent on the crisis trajectory, not just on the post-crisis input conditions.

**Stage 2 leverage note.** Apply to each 2026 reorganization. The diagnostic question is whether the change (a) required infrastructure capex, (b) required institutional commitment with switching costs, (c) created network effects with positive returns to scale. Affirmative on any of these suggests the change is hysteretic. UAE OPEC exit qualifies on (b) and (c); Saudi Red Sea pivot qualifies on (a); BRICS payments capex qualifies on all three. Conversely, oil price spikes are NOT hysteretic — Brent at $108 in May reverts to fundamentals once Hormuz reopens.

---

### 1.2 Chokepoint Capitalism Applied to Energy Trade

**Originator.** Adapting Rebecca Giblin and Cory Doctorow's framework on platform monopolies.

**Definition.** Geographic and infrastructural chokepoints (Hormuz, Suez, Bab-el-Mandeb, payment rails, undersea cables) can be *captured* for rent extraction. The chokepoint creates a structural monopoly leverable against trading parties. Whoever controls the chokepoint extracts rents independent of who *produces* the underlying value.

**Primary insight.** Once a chokepoint is captured and a toll regime established, alternative routes acquire infrastructure premium that persists even after the original chokepoint reopens.

**Stage 2 leverage note.** Iran's IRGC, by laying mines and creating its alternative shipping channel north of Larak Island and collecting yuan tolls (one ship paid $2 million for transit per Wikipedia "2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis"), demonstrated chokepoint capitalism at the geographic frontier. Stage 2 question: does the toll-paid channel *persist* after ceasefire as a parallel route with permanent insurance-rate premia? If yes, Hormuz transit returns to pre-war volumes only partially; the effective chokepoint becomes redistributed across multiple paths. The Saudi Red Sea pivot is the analog on the production side: once Aramco commits >$50B to Red Sea terminal expansion, the Hormuz dependency is structurally reduced regardless of what Iran does next.

---

### 1.3 Layered Deterrence Breakdown — Hysteretic Form

**Definition.** Once allies have invested in alternative-arrangement infrastructure (independent military integration, diversified suppliers, hedge alliances), restoring extended-deterrence credibility requires not just renewed US capability but *re-earning* allied confidence. The infrastructure itself is sunk cost the alliance now defends.

**Primary insight.** Allied hedging behavior, once operationalized via capex, persists even after the precipitating crisis ends.

**Stage 2 leverage note.** Three Stage 1 signals — UAE OPEC exit, Saudi Red Sea pivot, GCC military-integration calls — each carry sunk-cost commitments. Stage 2 question: which become permanent? Indicators: (1) Aramco capex commitments to Yanbu and Jeddah expansion at scale that requires 5–10 year payback; (2) UAE-Saudi joint procurement programs outside US-supplier channels; (3) GCC integrated air-defense network with non-US command-and-control architecture. Any of these crossing 24 months of operational standing converts the hedge into structural reorganization.

---

### 1.4 Strategic Petroleum Reserve / SPR Game Theory

**Definition.** SPRs are not just hedges against supply shock but bargaining chips in commodity-price coercion games. Drawdown rate signals expected war duration; replenishment trajectory signals expected post-war regime.

**Primary insight.** Post-crisis SPR rebuild paths reveal which actors expect renewed crisis on what timeline.

**Stage 2 leverage note.** Track US SPR replenishment rate post-ceasefire. Slow replenishment (<10M bbl/quarter) signals administration expects extended quiet; aggressive replenishment (>30M bbl/quarter) signals expectation of renewed conflict. China's pre-war stockpile build (~40-day inventory accumulation late 2025) is the comparison case — Chinese behavior post-ceasefire either continues accumulation (locked-in regional volatility expectation) or reverses (confidence in normalization). India's diversification (raised Russian crude purchases as Hormuz closed) is the third datapoint. If all three actors continue stockpile-building after Hormuz reopens, the energy-volatility regime is locked in regardless of Iran outcome.

## DOMAIN 2: Financial Architecture

### 2.1 Dollar Weaponization Fatigue / Reserve-Currency Erosion

**Definition.** Repeated coercive use of the dollar (Iran, Venezuela, Russia 2022, Afghanistan 2021) creates structural incentive for reserve diversification. Slow erosion (dollar share of reserves fell from ~73% in 2001 to roughly 54–58% by 2024–2025) with potential non-linear acceleration when threshold events occur.

**Primary insight.** Each weaponization episode adds a discrete increment to the diversification incentive. Central-bank gold purchases ran at 1,045 tonnes in 2024 (World Gold Council), the third consecutive year above 1,000 tonnes — the trend pre-dates Iran 2026 but the war is a discrete accelerant.

**Stage 2 leverage note.** Three reorganizations to assess for hysteresis: (1) Iran's Hormuz yuan-toll regime — does it persist as a "war remembrance" channel even after ceasefire, or do shippers revert to dollar settlement? (2) UAE's OPEC exit — does the UAE accept yuan/AED dual-pricing for its non-OPEC exports, locking in a non-dollar share? (3) BRICS Pay and mBridge — what fraction of pre-war dollar-denominated GCC-Asia trade has migrated to alternative rails by Q3? Threshold metric: yuan share of oil settlement crossing 12% sustained over two quarters indicates the diversification has crossed an irreversibility threshold.

### 2.2 Weaponized Interdependence — Reverse Form

**Originator.** Henry Farrell and Abraham Newman (*International Security*, 2019).

**Definition.** States with jurisdictional control over central network nodes (SWIFT, dollar clearing, semiconductor design, undersea cables) use those nodes for coercion via two mechanisms: the *panopticon effect* (extracting information by observing flows) and the *chokepoint effect* (denying adversaries access to flows). The reverse form: targets build alternative network infrastructure that, once operational, is itself a coercive instrument.

**Primary insight.** Globalization is hub-and-spoke, but hubs can be replicated. Replication takes years; once complete, the original hub's coercive premium collapses.

**Stage 2 leverage note.** SWIFT-cutting Iran, the secondary-sanctions architecture, and OFAC dominance are panopticon+chokepoint operations. But 2026 reveals the *self-undermining* dynamic Farrell and Newman warned about: BRICS Pay, mBridge, CIPS, China yuan-denominated oil tolls at Hormuz, and central-bank gold accumulation are explicit infrastructure responses. Stage 2 question: which of these crossed the operational threshold during the war? CIPS daily settlement volumes at 2x pre-war level by Q3 indicates lock-in. mBridge moving to production deployment with 4+ central banks indicates lock-in. Track Bank for International Settlements reporting and Chinese central bank disclosures.

### 2.3 Network Contagion / Financial-Political Crisis Transmission

**Definition.** Crises propagate across networks according to topology rather than fundamental coupling. Systemically important financial institutions and systemically important political institutions are nodes whose failure transmits non-locally.

**Primary insight.** The risk topology is network-structural. Once contagion paths are activated, they persist as latent transmission channels even after acute episodes resolve.

**Stage 2 leverage note.** GCC food-supply cascades during the war (Lulu Retail airlifts, 40–120% price spikes), Pakistani-Bangladeshi LPG shortages, Indian piped-gas substitution (580,000 new household connections in March 2026), African fertilizer constraints — all network-contagion patterns. Stage 2 question: which alternative-supply arrangements built during the crisis become permanent? Indicators: (1) Long-term LNG contracts signed during war between GCC and Indian/Bangladeshi buyers at non-Hormuz delivery points; (2) African fertilizer offtake agreements with Russian and Belarusian suppliers replacing GCC sources; (3) Indian energy diversification into permanent Russian-source dependency. Each represents hysteretic re-routing of the contagion network.

### 2.4 Reflexivity — Post-Crisis Form

**Originator.** George Soros.

**Definition.** Markets and the realities they price are linked by feedback. Post-crisis, the question becomes whether reflexive coupling that emerged during the crisis (e.g., Trump-statement-to-oil-price loops) persists as a structural feature or dissipates with policy normalization.

**Primary insight.** Reflexive coupling, once established, becomes a known feature of the policy environment. Market participants price the coupling itself, which can either reinforce or dampen the original loop.

**Stage 2 leverage note.** The three FT-documented oil-futures episodes (March 24, April 7, April 17, totaling ~$2.28B notional) priced political-financial coupling into the market structure. Stage 2 question: does the coupling persist post-ceasefire? Indicators: (1) Brent intraday volatility on Trump foreign-policy statements crossing pre-war baseline by Q3; (2) implied-volatility term structure on oil options pricing political risk premium beyond fundamental supply/demand; (3) CFTC enforcement disposition — whether referrals are made and whether prosecutions follow. If the CFTC declines criminal referral and approval continues, reflexive coupling is locked in as a market feature, not an episode.

## DOMAIN 3: Regime Political Settlements

### 3.1 Brittle Consolidation Paradox — Iranian Application

**Definition.** A regime under pressure may consolidate around a hardened core (IRGC triangle plus loyalist factions) while losing the constraining counterweights (pragmatist factions, technocratic ministry capacity, supreme-council legitimation). Consolidated weak factions operating under sustained pressure are not equivalent to consolidated strong factions.

**Primary insight.** Apparent regime resilience (as measured by absence of fragmentation) masks structural fragility (as measured by capacity to absorb further shocks). The consolidation is a hysteretic event: once pragmatists are sidelined, restoring them requires substantial regime concession.

**Stage 2 leverage note.** Mojtaba Khamenei's elevation via IRGC-pressured Assembly of Experts is a hysteretic consolidation. Stage 2 question: do pragmatist factions (Ghalibaf, Araghchi, Pezeshkian) reassert post-ceasefire, or has the IRGC triangle locked in regime structure for the next decade? Indicators: (1) Cabinet reshuffle composition post-ceasefire — IRGC-aligned vs. pragmatist appointments; (2) Khatam al-Anbiya economic empire expansion vs. contraction; (3) Pezeshkian's continued tenure or removal; (4) Assembly of Experts composition and authority signals. The regime's consolidation around vengeance over rescue (Foreign Affairs, "The New Khamenei": "members of the assembly appeared less interested in a leader to rescue the country from its current state of acute crisis than in a reincarnation of their 'martyred' leader to avenge his death") is the qualitative diagnosis; the institutional indicators are the quantitative confirmation.

### 3.2 Rentier State Theory — Wartime Update

**Originator.** Hossein Mahdavy (1970); Hazem Beblawi and Giacomo Luciani (1987); "new rentierism" updates (Brandeis Crown Center, 2020).

**Definition.** A state whose income derives from external rents (oil, gas, transit fees) rather than domestic taxation has distinctive dynamics: state autonomy from society, distributive legitimacy, suppressed productive sectors, high vulnerability to external rent shocks. The "new rentierism" extension: rather than buying stability, oil rents fund regional disruption.

**Primary insight.** Rentier states cannot easily reform out of sanctions because their political settlement is structured around rent distribution rather than productive bargaining with citizens. Wartime concentration of rentier flows through coercive channels (IRGC, Khatam al-Anbiya, bonyads) further entrenches the rentier-coercive coalition.

**Stage 2 leverage note.** The IRGC's economic empire is rentier political economy concentrated. Mojtaba's elevation, anchored in IRGC support, locks in the rentier-coercive coalition as the regime political settlement. Iran's Hormuz toll regime — IRGC charging foreign-flagged ships in yuan via Lloyd's-tracked payments — converted a transit chokepoint into a rentier income stream. Stage 2 hysteresis question: does the toll regime persist informally even after formal Hormuz reopening, with IRGC-affiliated entities collecting "passage fees" from shippers? If yes, the IRGC's rentier base is permanently expanded by the war.

### 3.3 Ontological Security

**Originator.** Jennifer Mitzen (*European Journal of International Relations*, 2006); Brent Steele (2008).

**Definition.** States seek not only physical survival but security of *self-conception* — a stable sense of who they are, achieved by routinized relationships and autobiographical narratives. States can become attached to dangerous routines that provide ontological security.

**Primary insight.** States may rationally choose conflict over peace if peace would require redefining their identity. The post-conflict identity question is whether new routines have replaced old ones.

**Stage 2 leverage note.** Iran's identity-as-resistance is so routinized that ontological security demands continued confrontation. Stage 2 question: does the regime's post-ceasefire posture exhibit mourning (acceptance of losses) or vengeance (continued mobilization)? Vengeance trajectory: continued IRGC operational signaling, "Martyrs of the Epic Struggle" iconography expansion, escalation of regional proxy support. Mourning trajectory: regime accommodation to economic constraints, partial pragmatist restoration, JCPOA-successor negotiation. Mirror image applies to MAGA: the war provides identity-confirming rituals valued regardless of strategic outcome. If post-ceasefire MAGA discourse continues to invoke Iran-war framing 6+ months after kinetic phase ends, the identity-confirmation function is locked in.

---

### 3.4 Memory Politics / Warped Mourning

**Originator.** Alexander Etkind (*Warped Mourning*, 2013).

**Definition.** Societies that fail to properly mourn political dead develop pathological politics in which trauma is endlessly reactivated as legitimating myth. Unprocessed political grief is a structural hazard.

**Primary insight.** Movements that build identity around martyrdom produce decision-making biased toward symbolic vengeance over material interest. The bias persists as long as the martyrology is institutionally maintained.

**Stage 2 leverage note.** The Iranian regime's reaction to Khamenei's killing — vengeance over rescue — is warped mourning operative in real time. The Shia martyrology (Karbala) provides a deep template; the "Soleimani 2020" memory politics is now compounded by Khamenei. Stage 2 indicators: (1) state-media and Friday-prayer iconography establishing Khamenei-as-martyr framing; (2) educational curriculum revisions; (3) anniversary commemorations and physical memorialization (the "Martyrs of the Epic Struggle" mural in Mashhad Ardehal is the leading example). Once the martyrology is institutionalized in curriculum and ritual, it becomes a multi-decade structural feature regardless of regime intentions.

## DOMAIN 4: US Institutional Residue

### 4.1 Constitutional Retrogression — Hysteretic Form

**Definition.** Detailed in Stage 1 catalog. Stage 2 application: assess which May 2026 maneuvers (WPR "hostilities terminated" certification, ceasefire-tolling doctrine, possible operation rebranding) survived the kinetic phase and have become baseline.

**Primary insight.** Retrogression events that are not legally challenged within their political moment become precedent. Post-crisis, the political energy for challenge dissipates; the legal precedent persists.

**Stage 2 leverage note.** The hysteresis test is whether any of the following occurred by Q3: (1) federal-court ruling on the WPR maneuver (negative ruling reverses; absence locks in); (2) AUMF passage that explicitly addresses the rebranding mechanism (would partially reverse); (3) bipartisan WPR amendment (would broadly reverse); (4) presidential commission or congressional investigation establishing the precedent character of the May 2026 maneuver. Absence of all four through end-Q3 means the maneuver has graduated from contested to baseline.

---

### 4.2 Competitive Authoritarianism — Post-Crisis Trajectory

**Originator.** Steven Levitsky and Lucan Way (2010, 2025).

**Definition.** A regime maintaining formally democratic institutions while systematically tilting them via state capture, selective enforcement, and intimidation. The post-crisis question: did the war produce a discrete consolidation episode that survives the war?

**Primary insight.** Foreign crises accelerate consolidation by providing legitimating cover. Post-crisis, the consolidation persists if it has built durable institutional infrastructure (statute, agency capture, judicial appointments, prosecutorial precedent).

**Stage 2 leverage note.** Indicators of consolidation lock-in: (1) DOJ and IRS pattern enforcement against Trump critics with national-security framings extending past kinetic phase; (2) FCC license actions against unfriendly media completed during war that hold post-war; (3) Title 50 expansions against domestic actors that survive judicial review; (4) Republican-Senate confirmation patterns (judicial appointments, IG removals) that lock in personnel for decade-plus tenures. Each individual event is defensible; the post-war pattern is the diagnosis.

---

### 4.3 Lawfare — Precedent Form

**Definition.** Detailed in Stage 1 catalog. Stage 2 application: which 2026 lawfare innovations entered the operational US legal toolkit, available for future deployment?

**Primary insight.** Successful lawfare innovations become reusable. Once an executive maneuver works without successful challenge, it is available to all future executives.

**Stage 2 leverage note.** Three lawfare innovations to track for graduation to baseline: (1) "Hostilities terminated" certification while operations continue — extends executive war-making authority by allowing the executive to define when WPR is in scope; (2) Ceasefire-tolling doctrine — pause-the-clock argument that, if not litigated, becomes available to future executives; (3) Operation rebranding to reset the WPR clock. The Stage 2 hysteresis test: have any of these been used in unrelated contexts (other regions, other operations) by Q3? Each cross-domain application confirms graduation from war-specific maneuver to general executive tool.

---

### 4.4 Wartime Political Economy — Post-Crisis Settlement

**Originator.** Sarah Kreps (2018); Risa Brooks; Rosella Cappella Zielinski.

**Definition.** Detailed in Stage 1 catalog. Stage 2 application: how was the war financed, and what does the post-war fiscal architecture lock in?

**Primary insight.** Off-budget war financing not only erodes wartime accountability; it produces fiscal residue (deferred costs, military reconstitution requirements, supplier-industry dependencies) that shapes the political economy for years.

**Stage 2 leverage note.** Three indicators of fiscal hysteresis: (1) Whether the $80–100B war supplemental was passed on-budget with offsets, off-budget without offsets, or deferred entirely; (2) Munitions reconstitution timeline (2027–2030) and which suppliers received locked-in multi-year contracts (Palantir-Anduril ~$30B Army contracts during war provide the baseline; track post-war expansion); (3) Whether wartime defense expansion is institutionalized via permanent line items or remains supplemental. Permanent line-item institutionalization is hysteretic — the elevated baseline becomes the new normal regardless of strategic environment.

## Caveats

The Stage 2 catalog is conditional on ceasefire or sustained kinetic-phase termination. If the kinetic phase resumes (via "Epic Passage" or "Project Freedom" rebranding), Stage 2 analysis is suspended and Stage 1 reactivates. The hysteresis assessment also requires patience: many indicators take 60–180 days post-ceasefire to resolve clearly. Premature locking-in or reversal calls produce false signals. The most reliable Stage 2 reads are made at six-month intervals from ceasefire date, not week-to-week.
