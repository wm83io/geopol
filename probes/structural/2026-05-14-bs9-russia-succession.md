## 2. Signal Cluster (Dec 2025 – May 2026)

Five hard signals constitute the trigger cluster. Each independently corroborated via T1/T2 sources per quality-sources.md.

**S1 — Serial general assassinations on Russian soil**  
Three senior officers killed inside Moscow since Dec 2024:
- Lt. Gen. Igor Kirillov, Dec 17, 2024 (scooter bomb; SBU claimed)
- Lt. Gen. Yaroslav Moskalik, April 2025 (car bomb)
- Lt. Gen. Fanil Sarvarov, Dec 22, 2025 (car bomb; third in sequence)

Dec 25, 2025: Putin held a restricted Security Council meeting on the Sarvarov aftermath. Representatives of security agencies blamed each other for protection failures. No consolidation; blame-trading.
*Source tier: T1 (Kremlin readout, Investigative Committee statements), T2 (PBS, NBC, Moscow Times)*

**S2 — Tsalikov arrest, March 5, 2026**  
Fourth Shoigu-era deputy minister arrested. Novelty: charged under "criminal organization" theory (12 counts embezzlement + money laundering + bribery), which creates legal predicate applicable to Shoigu directly. Tsalikov is the first to be charged as organizer rather than participant.  
Presidential Administration blocked Tsalikov's Federation Council candidacy in early 2025 on security-service recommendation — kompromat held in reserve, deployed at politically convenient moment.  
*Source tier: T1 (Investigative Committee statement), T2 (Moscow Times, Meduza), T3 (Jamestown Foundation)*

**S3 — EU intelligence dossier, May 4, 2026**  
Report from EU member-state intelligence agency, published by Important Stories (OCCRP partner), corroborated by CNN and New Voice of Ukraine. Key claims independently verified on several details:
- Kremlin on "high alert since beginning of March 2026" about coup/plot risk
- Putin fears drone assassination attempt "by members of the Russian political elite"
- Shoigu identified as "potential destabilizing actor" due to residual military command influence
- Tsalikov arrest characterized as "violation of informal guarantees of safety for elites"
- FSO mandate extended to 10 senior generals including 3 deputy chiefs of General Staff, at Gerasimov's request — confirming Gerasimov's political weight increased
*Source tier: T2 (CNN, OCCRP/Important Stories), independently corroborated*

**S4 — Putin behavioral signals**  
- Public appearances: ≥17 in 2025; 2 by late April 2026
- Extended stays in Krasnodar bunkers rather than Moscow/Valdai residences
- Staff surveillance: cooks, bodyguards, photographers banned from public transport; homes surveilled; visitors double-screened; only internet-free phones permitted near president
- Maduro capture (Jan 3, 2026) reported as specific escalation trigger for paranoia
*Source tier: T2 (FT European intel report via spotmedia; CNN dossier)*

**S5 — Economic structural break**  
Q1 2026 GDP: -0.3%. Full-year revised forecast: 0.4% (down from 1.3%). Oil/gas revenues: -47.1% YoY Jan-Feb 2026. NWF ~50% depleted. VAT raised 20→22%. Defense/security spending: 16.8T rubles planned (~$204B). Budget deficit reached 91% of full-year target in first two months.  
*Source tier: T2 (Moscow Times, Meduza, Euronews, CREA)*

## 3. Analytical Findings

### 3.1 Regime state diagnosis

The signal cluster is consistent with Stanovaya's "Wild Putinism" thesis (Carnegie, Nov 2024): the war has transformed the domestic political landscape such that no actor is safe and the informal rules governing elite competition have degraded. The system is in a self-reinforcing cycle: purge → anxiety → defection risk → purge. Putin's adaptive response (security tightening, kompromat deployment, isolation) increases his personal-continuity dependency, which amplifies the fragility it is designed to suppress.

Roman Anin (Important Stories publisher): *"We are witnessing the transition of the Russian regime into a fundamentally different state."* This framing, from the outlet that published and independently corroborated the EU dossier, is the most direct T2 characterization of the inflection.

### 3.2 Succession mechanism probabilities (5-year)

| Mechanism | Probability | Key constraint |
|---|---|---|
| M1: Managed transit | 55-65% | No designated successor; Putin unwilling to name one; window narrows as age increases |
| M2: Palace action | 8-12% | No focal point; Zolotov as loyalist reaction force; asymmetric punishment deterrent (though decaying) |
| M3: Incapacity cascade | 20-30% | Age 73; behavioral isolation; no pre-coordination among factions; constitutional 90-day window too short |
| M4: War-loss cascade | <5% | Battlefield ceiling prevents conventional defeat; but tail not zero |

M3 range elevated from "unmodeled" to 20-30% based on signal cluster. M2 elevated from "unmodeled" to 8-12%.

### 3.3 Posture continuity under M1

Near-total. MIC employment (3.8M+), nuclear doctrine (Nov 2024 revision), hybrid toolkit, and China dependency are all institutionally embedded. Any M1 successor inherits them as structural constraints, not policy choices.

### 3.4 Posture under M3

Highest variance. Mishustin as constitutional default lacks faction backing. 90-day bargaining window under trust-collapse conditions. China moves to lock in dependency terms. NATO reads it as an opportunity window. Nuclear signaling credibility gap: successor inherits doctrine but not the personal deterrent reputation, creating pressure to demonstrate resolve precisely when instability makes escalation most dangerous.

### 3.5 China-pole acceleration under any succession

Any succession path weakens Russia's bargaining position with China. A transitional government needs Chinese economic support and will be in a weak position to resist Chinese terms on energy pricing, infrastructure, and yuan-settlement expansion. Long-run absorption trajectory (Russia-adjacent pole → junior partner within China-centered pole) accelerates regardless of which mechanism obtains.

### 3.6 Iran 2026 framework implication

A weakening or transitioning Russia is net-negative for Iran. Fork B-Russia (≤5% in v3.0) approaches zero under M3. PROBE-6 (China-as-sole-external-brake) reading reinforced. Russia's role degrades from supportive secondary pole to unreliable junior partner.

## 5. What Would Falsify This Reading

BS-9 elevation should be revised downward if:
- Putin public appearances normalize to >10/month for 3+ consecutive months
- Shoigu rehabilitation (return to operational portfolio with real authority)
- Credible managed handover announcement with institutional backing
- Brent sustained $80+ for 2+ quarters
- New strategic arms agreement replacing New START

- Additional general assassinations inside Russia
- Shoigu criminal prosecution formally initiated
- Second inter-siloviki blame episode (repeat of Dec 25, 2025 pattern)
- Kadyrov incapacity confirmed
- Ukraine battlefield reversal of significant territory
